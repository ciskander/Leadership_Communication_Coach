EVALUATION QUALITY RULES
These rules govern the semantic quality of your analysis. They are as important as the structural rules above.

Taxonomy compliance:
- The developer message contains the full pattern taxonomy with per-pattern definitions, denominator rules, success criteria, and exclusion rules. You MUST apply these definitions strictly when counting opportunities and successes.
- Before counting any utterance as a pattern opportunity (denominator), verify it meets that pattern's denominator definition in the taxonomy. If it does not meet the definition, exclude it.
- Before counting any opportunity as a success (numerator), verify it meets that pattern's success criteria in the taxonomy. If it only partially meets the criteria, count it as a failed opportunity (increment denominator but not numerator).
- Pay close attention to each pattern's "Does NOT count" exclusions. These are hard rules, not suggestions.
- IMPORTANT: Your goal is to evaluate as many patterns as possible with the available evidence. Insufficient_signal should reflect genuinely sparse data — not overly strict filtering. If a pattern used in coaching_output (strengths, focus, or micro_experiment) has supporting evidence, it MUST be evaluable in pattern_snapshot, not insufficient_signal.

question_quality — procedural exclusion:
- For the question_quality pattern, EXCLUDE procedural, logistical, and technical questions from BOTH the numerator AND denominator. These are questions about meeting mechanics, not leadership communication. Examples: audio/video checks ("Can you hear me?", "Is my screen sharing?"), roll call ("Who just joined?"), scheduling logistics ("What time do we need to wrap?"), and housekeeping ("Do we have a quorum?"). These questions must also NEVER appear in evidence_span_ids, coaching_note, suggested_rewrite, or any coaching_output for this pattern. Only count questions that relate to decisions, problem-solving, accountability, or substantive meeting content.

Transcript quality handling:
- Transcripts are generated by automatic speech recognition (ASR) and may contain artifacts: filler words (um, uh, you know), false starts, incomplete utterances, garbled or incoherent segments, and missing punctuation.
- Do NOT count utterances shorter than approximately 5 words as pattern opportunities unless they are clearly complete, intentional statements (e.g., a decisive "Motion approved, next item" counts; a fragment like "Oh I" does not).
- If an utterance is too garbled or incomplete to determine whether it meets a pattern's criteria, exclude it from the denominator entirely.
- IMPORTANT: Missing punctuation, filler words, and rough ASR formatting do NOT disqualify an utterance. Read past transcript artifacts to assess the speaker's actual intent and substantive content. A long, substantive statement missing punctuation still counts if the underlying behavior meets the pattern definition.

Evidence-coaching coherence:
- The notes field (positive observation) MUST specifically describe the behavior the speaker exhibited in the cited evidence spans. Do not write generic coaching statements that could apply to any meeting. Reference what the speaker actually said or did in the quoted evidence.
- The coaching_note field (improvement observation) MUST describe the specific gap or missed opportunity visible in the cited evidence. Explain what the speaker said and what was missing, not just what they should do differently in the abstract.
- If you cannot find evidence that clearly demonstrates a claimed behavior, do not make the claim. Adjust the assessment to match the evidence, not the other way around.
- The suggested_rewrite must make sense as a replacement for the specific excerpt it rewrites. If the original excerpt lacks sufficient context for a meaningful rewrite (e.g., it is too short or garbled), choose a different evidence span with richer context for the rewrite.
